Nikola Tesla

"A person who changed the lives of billions of people for the better"

  • 1856 - Born in Smiljan, Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia).
  • 1875 - Enrolls at the Austrian Polytechnic in Graz, where he studies electrical engineering.
  • 1881 - Moves to Budapest, Hungary, and works at the Central Telephone Exchange, where he develops the concept of the induction motor.
  • 1882 - Moves to Paris and works for the Continental Edison Company, improving electrical equipment.
  • 1884 - Emigrates to the United States and begins working for Thomas Edison in New York City.
  • 1885 - Leaves Edison’s company after a disagreement over payment and founds Tesla Electric Light & Manufacturing.
  • 1887 - Develops the alternating current (AC) induction motor and begins working with George Westinghouse.
  • 1888 - Patents the AC motor and transformer, which become foundational for modern electrical systems.
  • 1891 - Invents the Tesla coil, a device essential for wireless transmission of electricity.
  • 1893 - Demonstrates wireless power transmission and AC electricity at the World’s Columbian Exposition in Chicago.
  • 1895 - Designs one of the first hydroelectric power plants at Niagara Falls, showcasing the potential of AC power.
  • 1899 - Moves to Colorado Springs, where he conducts experiments with high-voltage electricity and wireless transmission.
  • 1901 - Begins construction of the Wardenclyffe Tower on Long Island, aiming to create a global wireless communication system.
  • 1906 - Invents the bladeless turbine, a device for fluid propulsion.
  • 1915 - Files a lawsuit against Guglielmo Marconi over patent infringement related to radio technology.
  • 1943 - Dies in New York City at the age of 86. His contributions to electrical engineering and wireless technology remain influential
